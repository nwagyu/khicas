5.5.4  Decomposition into prime factors: ifactor

The ifactor command factors an integer into its prime factors. (Note that a prime factor of a Gaussian integer is only determined up to a factor of ±1 or ± i.)

  • ifactor takes one argument:
    n, an integer or a list of integers.
  • ifactor(n) returns n in factored form (or a list of the integers in factored form).

Examples

  • Input:
    ifactor(90)
    Output:
      5· 2· 32
  • Input:
    ifactor(-90)
    Output:
      −5· 2· 32
  • Input:
    ifactor(14+23*i)
    Output:
      i ⎛
    ⎝    		2−i⎞
    ⎠    		2 ⎛
    ⎝    		5+2 i⎞
    ⎠
  • Input:
    ifactor([36,52])
    Output:
      ⎡
    ⎣    		22· 32,13· 22⎤
    ⎦
