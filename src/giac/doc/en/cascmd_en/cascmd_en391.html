5.35.5  Determinant of a matrix with coefficients in ℤ/pℤ: Det

In Xcas mode, Det is simply the inert form of det; namely, it gives the determinant of a matrix without evaluating it. (See Section 5.47.4.) In Maple mode, the Det command can additionally be used in conjunction with mod to find the determinant of a matrix whose elements are in ℤ/pℤ.

  • (In Maple mode.)
    Det takes one argument:
    A, a matrix with elements in ℤ/pℤ.
  • Det(A) returns the determinant of A.

Example

Det([[1,2,9] mod 13,[3,10,0] mod 13,[3,11,1] mod 13])

To find the value of the determinant, you can enter:
Input:

eval(ans())

Output:

5%13

Hence, in ℤ/13ℤ, the determinant of A=[[1, 2, 9],[3,10,0],[3,11,1]] is 5%13 (in ℤ, det(A)=31).
Input (in Maple mode):

Det([[1,2,9],[3,10,0],[3,11,1]]) mod 13

Output:

5
