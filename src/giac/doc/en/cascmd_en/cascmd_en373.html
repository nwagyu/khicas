5.34.6  Euclidean remainder: rem

The rem command finds the remainder of the Euclidean division of two polynomials (see also Section 5.28.3).

  • rem takes two mandatory arguments and one optional argument:
    • P and Q, two polynomials with coefficients in ℤ/pℤ.
    • Optionally x, the variable (by default x), if P and Q are given as expressions.
  • rem(P,Q  ⟨,x⟩) returns the remainder of the Euclidean division of P divided by Q.

Example

Input:

rem((x^3+x^2+1)%13,(2*x^2+4)%13)

Output:

⎛
⎝		⎛
⎝		−2⎞
⎠		%13⎞
⎠		x+⎛
⎝		−1⎞
⎠		%13

Indeed x3+x2+1=(2x2+4)(x+1/2)+5x−4/4 and −3*4=−6*2=1 mod13.
