5.27.6  Transforming a polynomial in internal format into a list, and conversely: convert

The convert command does many conversions (see Section 5.23.26). Among other things, it can convert between a polynomial in internal sparse multivariate format and a list representing the polynomial.

To convert from a polynomial in internal sparse multivariate format to a list:

  • convert takes one mandatory argument and one optional argument:
    • P, a polynomial written in internal sparse multivariate format (see Section 5.27.2).
    • Optionally, list.
  • convert(P ⟨, list⟩) returns a list representing the polynomial.

Example

Input:

p:= symb2poly(x^2 - x*y + 2y, [x,y])

Output:

%%%{1,[2,0]%%%}+%%%{-1,[1,1]%%%}+%%%{2,[0,1]%%%}

l:= convert(p,list)

To convert from a list representing a polynomial to the polynomial in internal sparse multivariate format:

  • convert takes two arguments:
    • L, a list representing a polynomial.
    • polynom.
  • convert(L,polynom) returns the polynomial in internal sparse multivariate format (see Section 5.27.2).
