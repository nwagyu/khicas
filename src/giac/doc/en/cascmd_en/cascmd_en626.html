8.1.1  The mean: mean

Recall that the mean of a list x1,…,xn is simply their numeric average (x1 + ⋯ + xn)/n. The mean command finds the mean of a list.

  • mean takes one mandatory argument and one optional argument:
    • L, a list or matrix of numbers.
    • W, a list or matrix of weights, the same size as L.
  • mean(L ⟩ W⟨) returns the mean of the list or a list with the means of the columns of the matrix.

Examples

  • Input:
    mean([1,2,3,4])
    Output:
    5
    2
    since (1+2+3+4)/4 = 5/2.
  • Input:
    mean([[1,2,3],[5,6,7]])
    Output:
    ⎡
    ⎣    		3,4,5⎤
    ⎦
    since (1+5)/2 = 3, (2+6)/2 = 4 and (3 + 7)/2 = 5.
  • Input:
    mean([2,4,6,8],[2,2,3,3])
    Output:
    27
    5
    since (2· 2 + 4· 2 + 6· 3 + 8· 3)/(2 + 2 + 3 + 3) = 27/5.
  • Input:
    mean([[1,2],[3,4]],[[1,2],[2,1]])
    Output:
    ⎡
    ⎢
    ⎢
    ⎣
    7
    3
    		,
    8
    3
    		⎤
    ⎥
    ⎥
    ⎦
    since (1· 1 + 3· 2)/(1+2) = 7/3 and (2· 2 + 4· 1)/(2 + 1) = 8/3.
