5.9.4  Product of cycles to permutation: cycles2permu

The cycles2permu command is the inverse of perm2cycles; it turns a sequence of cycles into a permutation.

  • cycles2permu takes one argument:
    c, a list of cycles.
  • cycles2permu(c) returns the permutation (of size n chosen as small as possible) that is the product of the given cycles.

Examples

  • Input:
    cycles2permu([[1,3,5],[2,4]])
    Output:
      ⎡
    ⎣    		0,3,4,5,2,1⎤
    ⎦
  • Input:
    cycles2permu([[2,4]])
    Output:
      ⎡
    ⎣    		0,1,4,3,2⎤
    ⎦
  • Input:
    cycles2permu([[5],[2,4]])
    Output:
      ⎡
    ⎣    		0,1,4,3,2,5⎤
    ⎦
