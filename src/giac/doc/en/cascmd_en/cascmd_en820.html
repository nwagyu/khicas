13.9.3  Rectangles in the plane: rectangle

See Section 14.8.3 for rectangles in space.

The rectangle creates rectangles.

  • rectangle takes three mandatory arguments and two optional arguments:
    • A,B, two points.
    • k, a nonzero real number.
    • Optionally, varc, vard, two variable names.
  • rectangle(A,B,k ⟨varc,vard⟩) returns and draws the rectangle ABCD, where AD=|k|· AB and the angle from AB to AD is counterclockwise if k > 0, clockwise if k<0.
    If the arguments varc and vard are given, then C and D will be assigned to them.

Given rectangle(A,B,k), Xcas computes D by affix(D) = affix(A) + kexp(iπ/2)(affix(B)−affix(A)). If k is complex, then rectangle draws a parallelogram.
