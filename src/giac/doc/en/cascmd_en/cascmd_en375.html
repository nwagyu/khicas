5.34.8  Division in ℤ/pℤ or in ℤ/pℤ[x]: /

The / operator divides two integers in ℤ/pℤ or two polynomials A and B in ℤ/pℤ[x]. (See also Section 5.8.2.) Since ℤ/pℤ is only a field if p is prime, the quotient is only guaranteed to exist if p is prime (unless the denominator is 0 (mod p )).

  • For integers in ℤ/pℤ:

    Example

    • Input:
      5%13/2% 13
      Since 13 is prime, you get:
      Output:
          ⎛
      ⎝      		−4⎞
      ⎠      		%13
    • Input:
      5%14/3% 14
      Since 3(mod 1 )4 is invertible in Z/14ℤ, you get:
      Output:
        ⎛
      ⎝      		−3⎞
      ⎠      		%14
    • Input:
      5%14/7% 14
      Since 7(mod 1 )4 is not invertible in Z/14ℤ, you will get an error: Output:
      Not invertible Error: Bad Argument Value
  • For polynomials, the result of P/Q is its irreducible representation in ℤ/pℤ[x].

    Example

    Input:

    (2*x^2+5)%13/(5*x^2+2*x-3)%13

    Output:

      
    ⎛
    ⎝    		6%13⎞
    ⎠    		x+1%13
    ⎛
    ⎝    		2%13⎞
    ⎠    		x+⎛
    ⎝    		2%13⎞
    ⎠    		%13
