5.42.1  Norms of a vector: maxnorm l1norm l2norm norm

There are different norms for vectors in ℝn, and Xcas has different commands for them.

Given a list L=[a1,…,an],

  • maxnorm(L) returns the l∞ norm of L, namely max(|a1|,|a2|,…,|an|)

    Example

    Input:

    maxnorm([3,-4,2])

    Output:

    4

    Indeed, 4=max(|3|,|−4|,|2|).

  • l1norm(L) returns the l1 norm of L, namely |a1|+|a2|+⋯+|an|. coordinates.

    Example

    Input:

    l1norm([3,-4,2])

    Output:

    9

    Indeed, 9=|3|+|−4|+|2|.

  • norm(L) or l2norm(L) returns the ℓ2 norm of L; namely √|a1|2+|a2|2+⋯+|an|2.

    Example

    Input:

    norm([3,-4,2])

    Output:

    √
    29

    Indeed, 29=|3|2+|−4|2+|2|2.
