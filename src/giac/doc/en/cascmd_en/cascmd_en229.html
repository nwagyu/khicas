5.20.3  Discrete summation: sum

The sum command can evaluate sums, series, and find discrete antiderivatives. A discrete antiderivative of a sum ∑nf(n) is an expression G such that G|x=n+1−G|x=n=f(n), which means that ∑n=MNf(n) = G|x=N+1−G|M.


To evaluate a sum or series:

  • sum takes four arguments:
    • expr, an expression.
    • k, the name of the variable.
    • n0 and n1, integers (the bounds of the sum).
  • sum(expr,k,n0,n1) returns the sum ∑k=n0n1expr.

Examples

  • Input:
    sum(1,k,-2,n)
    Output:
    n+1+2
  • Input:
    normal(sum(2*k-1,k,1,n))
    Output:
    n2
  • Input:
    sum(1/(n^2),n,1,10)
    Output:
    1968329
    1270080

To find a discrete antiderivative:

  • sum takes two arguments:
    • expr, an expression.
    • k, the name of the variable.
  • sum(expr,x) returns a discrete antiderivative.

Example

Input:

sum(1/(x*(x+1)),x)

Output:

−
1
