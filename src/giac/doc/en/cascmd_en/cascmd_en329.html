5.28.5  GCD of two polynomials with the Euclidean algorithm: gcd Gcd

The gcd command computes the gcd (greatest common divisor) of polynomials. (See also Section 5.5.1 for GCD of integers.)

  • gcd takes an unspecified number or arguments:
    polys, a sequence or list of polynomials.
  • gcd(polys) returns the greatest common divisor of the polynomials in polys.

Examples

  • Input:
    gcd(x^2+2*x+1,x^2-1)
    Output:
      x+1
  • Input:
    gcd(x^2-2*x+1,x^3-1,x^2-1,x^2+x-2)
    or:
    gcd([x^2-2*x+1,x^3-1,x^2-1,x^2+x-2])
    Output:
      x−1
  • For polynomials with modular coefficients:
    Input (e.g.):
    gcd((x^2+2*x+1) mod 5,(x^2-1) mod 5)
    Output:
      ⎛
    ⎝    		1%5⎞
    ⎠    		x+1%5
